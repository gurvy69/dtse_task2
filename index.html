What is a Prediction Market?

A prediction market is a financial market designed to aggregate information and beliefs about future events. Participants buy and sell contracts that pay out based on the outcome of specified events.

1Event Definition

Each market is based on a specific, verifiable event with clear resolution criteria. Events can be binary (Yes/No) or have multiple outcomes.

Examples "Will Apple release an AI-powered iPhone by June 2025?"
"Will India's GDP growth exceed 7% in 2025?"
"Will Bitcoin reach $100,000 before December 2025?"

2Price = Probability

Share prices range from $0.00 to $1.00, representing the market's collective probability estimate. A "Yes" share trading at $0.65 means the market believes there's a 65% chance the event will occur.

Example If a market shows $0.22 for "Yes": The crowd believes there's a 22% chance the event will happen.

3Trading & Profit

Buy low, sell high—or hold until settlement. If you believe an event is more likely than the current price suggests, you buy "Yes" shares. If you think it's less likely, you buy "No" shares or sell "Yes" shares.

Example You buy 100 "Yes" shares at $0.30 each ($30 total). The event happens. You receive $100 (100 shares × $1). Your profit: $70.

4Settlement

When the event occurs or the deadline passes, contracts are settled. "Yes" shares pay $1.00 if the outcome is true, $0.00 if false. "No" shares pay $1.00 if false, $0.00 if true.

Example Transactions

Tech Product Launch

Event: "Will Apple announce a foldable iPhone in 2025?"

Initial Price: Yes shares at $0.15 (15% probability)

Your Action: You've read patent filings and supply chain reports. You believe it's underpriced. You buy 200 Yes shares at $0.15 each = $30 investment.

Price Movement: After credible leaks from a reliable source, the price rises to $0.45.

Option A - Sell Early: You sell at $0.45 = 200 × $0.45 = $90. Profit: $60 (200% return)

Option B - Hold to Settlement: Apple announces the foldable iPhone. Yes shares pay $1.00. You receive $200. Profit: $170 (567% return)

Economic Indicator

Event: "Will India's Q4 2025 GDP growth exceed 7.5%?"

Initial Price: Yes shares at $0.70 (70% probability)

Your Analysis: Economic data suggests cooling growth. You think the market is overconfident. You buy 150 No shares at $0.30 each = $45 investment.

Outcome: Official GDP data shows 7.2% growth—below the 7.5% threshold.

Settlement: Your No shares pay $1.00 each = 150 × $1.00 = $150. Profit: $105 (233% return)

Geopolitical Event

Event: "Will a major trade agreement be signed between India and the EU by June 2025?"

Initial Price: Yes shares at $0.55 (55% probability)

Your Position: You buy 100 Yes shares at $0.55 = $55 investment.

News Impact: Negotiations stall over agricultural subsidies. Price drops to $0.25.

Your Decision: You sell early at $0.25 to minimize losses = 100 × $0.25 = $25. Loss: $30 (but you preserved $25 of your capital instead of losing it all)
